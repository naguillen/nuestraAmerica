Origins

Nuestra America Student Club was founded at Lowell High School in San Francsico, CA in 2012 by Nathalie and Ileana Guillen, two sisters of Peruvian ancestry who saw the need for a club that provided a safe, educational space for Latino students at the school. The goal of Nuestra America is to empower and convey to Latino American students knowledge of their roots, history and culture.

Vision

Nuestra America Student Club's founding was inspired by the movement started by various Latin American countries to reclaim their identity, acquire social justice for their people, freedom from economic debt in addition to regaining their status as a strong united continent.

Objective

The club serves as a space where students can come to learn of the great cultural tapestry we hold, to submerge themselves in the knowledge of our rich past as well as develop a sense of belonging, explore future goals etc. Our vision when we started the group was to analyze our present status as Latinos in the United States by becoming aware of current events, policies, legislations and other issues that affect our community. Proudly, the members of Nuestra America can say that we are the only club at Lowell HS with a bilingual bulletin board that publicizes ethnic specific news, poems, resources, know-your-rights information and the like.
